# Intake Question Bank

Ask only the smallest set of questions needed to avoid a generic strategy. Default: 3-7 questions.
If the user asks for no questions or wants immediate output, proceed with assumptions and label them.

## Universal high-information questions

Choose from these first:

1. What exact outcome are we optimizing for, and by when? Include a number if possible.
2. Who is the target customer/user/audience, and what painful job are they trying to get done?
3. What is the offer/product/service, price point, and main reason someone would choose it now?
4. What stage are we in: idea, pre-launch, early traction, PMF, scale, mature, turnaround?
5. What traction or data do we already have: revenue, users, conversion, CAC, retention, pipeline, win rate?
6. What has already been tried, and what happened?
7. What are the real constraints: budget, time, team, brand, legal, product quality, sales capacity, geography?
8. What assets do we already have: audience, customer list, case studies, data, founder credibility, partners, content, product usage?
9. What would be an ambitious but believable “wow” outcome?
10. What is off-limits: channels, brand tone, tactics, markets, price moves, risk level?

## Marketing questions

- Is the issue awareness, demand capture, conversion, trust, retention, or all of the above?
- Which channels currently produce revenue or qualified leads?
- What is the current funnel: impressions → clicks → leads → calls → customers → repeat?
- What proof do we have: case studies, testimonials, demos, before/after, data, credentials?
- What is the primary buying trigger?

## GTM questions

- What are we launching or expanding, and into which segment/geography/use case?
- Is this a new product, new market, new buyer, or new channel?
- What is the beachhead segment most likely to feel urgent pain?
- What sales motion is realistic: self-serve, founder-led, inside sales, enterprise, channel, PLG?
- What must be true in the first 30/90 days to call the GTM successful?

## Business strategy questions

- What business are we really in: product, service, marketplace, platform, media, agency, SaaS, ecommerce, local, enterprise?
- Where do profits come from now, and where could they come from in the future?
- What advantage can we build that competitors cannot copy quickly?
- Which market/customer/product line deserves more capital, and which should be starved or killed?
- What strategic constraint is most binding: demand, supply, product, unit economics, capability, capital, regulation, competition?

## Growth questions

- What is the North Star or primary growth metric?
- Where is the biggest drop-off: acquisition, activation, retention, referral, revenue?
- What is the current retention curve/cohort behavior?
- Are there natural loops: content, referral, collaboration, marketplace, data, community, paid?
- How many experiments can the team run per week without quality collapsing?

## Product questions

- What user behavior proves the product delivered value?
- What is the “aha” moment and how quickly does it happen?
- Which segment loves the product most, even if small?
- What are the top three user complaints, workarounds, or feature requests?
- What must the product stop doing to become sharper?

## Brand/positioning questions

- What category do customers currently place you in?
- Who is the enemy: old way, competitor, status quo, waste, complexity, risk?
- What is your one believable point of difference?
- What points of parity must you satisfy to be trusted?
- What proof makes the claim credible?

## Sales questions

- Who signs, who uses, who blocks, and who pays?
- What is the current pipeline by stage and conversion rate?
- What are the top objections and where do deals stall?
- Is the main issue prospecting, qualification, discovery, demo, proposal, negotiation, or follow-up?
- What ACV/price and sales cycle are we dealing with?

## Pricing questions

- What is the customer’s next-best alternative and what does it cost?
- Which value metric grows with customer value: seats, usage, volume, GMV, credits, outcomes, features?
- What segments have different willingness to pay?
- What happens when price increases: churn, downgrades, lower conversion, better positioning?
- Are discounts strategic, reactive, or uncontrolled?

## Fundraising questions

- Stage, amount raising, runway, current traction, and milestone after the round?
- Why now: market timing, regulation, technology shift, behavior change?
- What unique advantage does the team have?
- What proof makes this more than an idea?
- What is the biggest investor objection?

## Marketplace/platform questions

- Which side is harder to acquire: supply or demand?
- What is the atomic network: the smallest niche where liquidity can be high?
- What is the liquidity metric: time to match, fill rate, search success, utilization, repeat transactions?
- What trust/safety risk could break the marketplace?
- What incentives or subsidies are available for cold start?

## Community/partnership questions

- What business objective should the community or partnership drive: acquisition, retention, support, advocacy, product feedback, ecosystem revenue?
- What does the partner/member get that they cannot easily get elsewhere?
- What behaviors create compounding value?
- What governance, incentives, or moderation are needed?
- How will we prove business impact without vanity metrics?
