# Output Rubric

Use this rubric before finalizing. Do not show the score unless the user asks for evaluation.
If the output is weak, revise it silently and answer with the improved version.

Score each 0-3:

1. Diagnosis quality
- 0: no diagnosis
- 1: generic diagnosis
- 2: plausible bottleneck
- 3: sharp diagnosis that changes the action plan

2. Strategic choice
- 0: list of options only
- 1: weak recommendation
- 2: clear choice with some rationale
- 3: clear choice plus explicit rejected alternatives and trade-offs

3. Context specificity
- 0: could apply to any company
- 1: lightly customized
- 2: uses user's context well
- 3: feels built for this exact case, with assumptions labeled

4. Execution detail
- 0: slogans
- 1: broad tactics
- 2: concrete actions
- 3: sequenced plan with owners/cadence/examples/first actions

5. Measurement
- 0: no metrics
- 1: generic metrics
- 2: relevant metrics
- 3: leading/lagging metrics, thresholds, and learning cadence

6. Feasibility
- 0: unrealistic
- 1: ignores constraints
- 2: mostly feasible
- 3: fits budget, stage, team, and timing constraints

7. Creativity/leverage
- 0: obvious advice only
- 1: one mild idea
- 2: useful non-obvious angles
- 3: high-upside moves that are creative but realistic

8. Risk and uncertainty
- 0: no risk
- 1: generic risks
- 2: relevant risks
- 3: risks tied to assumptions with mitigations/kill criteria

9. Anti-fluff
- 0: full of generic statements
- 1: some generic claims
- 2: mostly concrete
- 3: no generic claim appears without operational detail

## Passing threshold

- 24+ out of 27: strong.
- 20-23: acceptable; tighten specificity.
- Under 20: rewrite before final.

## Red flags that require rewriting

- “Post consistently,” “create value,” “know your audience,” “optimize the funnel,” or “build community” appear without exact operationalization.
- The output lists channels without explaining why those channels fit the target and offer.
- The plan lacks a first 72-hour action list.
- There is no “do not do” section.
- The strategy depends on current facts but presents assumptions as facts.
- The answer has frameworks but no decision.
- The answer gives tactics before naming the bottleneck.

## Final answer style

Use crisp headings. Be direct. Say what to do, what not to do, and why.
Prefer a small number of high-conviction moves over a large menu of possibilities.
