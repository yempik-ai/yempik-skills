# Research Protocol

Use this protocol when the strategy depends on current, external, or niche facts.

## Research is required when the user asks about

- current market trends, platforms, algorithms, ad rules, SEO changes, app-store rules, social media changes;
- competitor positioning, pricing, features, funding, hiring, partnerships, or recent launches;
- legal/regulatory context, taxes, financial products, medical claims, privacy, employment, consumer protection;
- market sizing, benchmarks, CAC, CPM, conversion benchmarks, salary costs, tool pricing, macro conditions;
- “latest,” “today,” “now,” “2026,” “what works currently,” or time-sensitive opportunities.

## If tools are available

1. Search primary/authoritative sources first: official docs, company pages, filings, reputable research, platform documentation.
2. Cross-check key claims with at least two independent sources where possible.
3. Separate facts, estimates, and interpretations.
4. Cite sources when presenting factual claims.
5. Use recent sources for time-sensitive topics.
6. Do not overfit strategy to a single article or guru claim.

## If tools are not available

Say something like:

“I can build a strong hypothesis-based strategy, but current competitor/platform/market facts should be verified before execution. I will label assumptions and show what to research first.”

Then proceed with a useful strategy rather than stopping.

## Research output structure

When research matters, include:

- Known facts.
- Unverified assumptions.
- Strategic implications.
- What must be validated before spending serious money.
- How to validate quickly.

## Data quality hierarchy

1. User-provided internal data.
2. Primary sources: official docs, company websites, filings, analytics exports, CRM data.
3. Reputable industry research and expert publications.
4. Credible news sources.
5. Anecdotal/social sources, used only as weak signals.

## Never invent

Do not invent market size, competitor pricing, platform rules, conversion benchmarks, legal requirements,
or financial performance. If unknown, say unknown and create a validation plan.
