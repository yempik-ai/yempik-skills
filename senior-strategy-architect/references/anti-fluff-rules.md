# Anti-Fluff Rules

This file defines what “no bullshit” means for strategy outputs.

## Banned generic advice unless operationalized

Do not say these as standalone advice:

- “Post consistently.”
- “Create valuable content.”
- “Know your audience.”
- “Optimize the funnel.”
- “Build a community.”
- “Leverage influencers.”
- “Use storytelling.”
- “Focus on quality.”
- “Test and learn.”
- “Improve your branding.”
- “Run ads.”
- “Do SEO.”
- “Differentiate yourself.”
- “Provide excellent customer service.”
- “Use data.”

If one of these is relevant, convert it into an operational prescription.

## Replacement patterns

Instead of “post consistently,” say:
- exact content pillars;
- cadence;
- channel;
- format;
- example titles/hooks;
- production workflow;
- metric;
- kill/iterate threshold.

Instead of “optimize the funnel,” say:
- which stage is leaking;
- current vs target conversion;
- hypotheses;
- experiments;
- instrumentation;
- decision threshold.

Instead of “differentiate,” say:
- target;
- frame of reference;
- point of parity;
- point of difference;
- reason to believe;
- competitor to avoid copying;
- proof asset to create.

Instead of “build community,” say:
- business objective;
- member identity;
- member value;
- recurring ritual;
- contribution loop;
- moderation/governance;
- business metric.

Instead of “run ads,” say:
- audience;
- offer;
- creative angle;
- landing page promise;
- budget test size;
- success threshold;
- follow-up sequence.

## Senior strategy language

Prefer:
- “The bottleneck is…”
- “I would not…”
- “The highest-leverage move is…”
- “This only works if…”
- “The first proof point to get is…”
- “Kill this if…”
- “The trade-off is…”

Avoid:
- “It depends” without a decision.
- “Consider doing X, Y, Z” with no priority.
- “There are many ways…” as filler.
- “Best practices” without adapting to context.
- Long inspirational openings.

## Specificity test

After drafting each major recommendation, ask:

“Could this advice be pasted into a different business without changing much?”

If yes, make it more specific or remove it.
