# Fundraising and Investor Narrative Playbook

Use for pitch decks, fundraising strategy, investor story, seed/Series A narrative, market sizing,
traction framing, milestones, use of funds, or investor objections.

## First diagnosis

Identify the fundraising bottleneck:

- Problem is not urgent or painful enough.
- Market does not feel large or timely.
- Solution sounds like a feature, not a company.
- Traction is present but not framed as a wedge.
- Team advantage is unclear.
- Business model or go-to-market is vague.
- Round size and milestones do not connect.
- Investor objection is obvious but unanswered.

## Required strategic choices

- Investor target: angel, seed fund, sector specialist, growth investor, strategic, local ecosystem.
- Round objective: what milestone the capital achieves.
- Narrative spine: problem → insight → solution → why now → market → traction → model → team → ask.
- Proof hierarchy: what is proven, what is assumed, what will be proven next.
- Wedge: the first market/use case that expands into a bigger vision.
- Use of funds: tied to milestones, not generic hiring.

## Output must include

- Fundraising diagnosis.
- Narrative thesis.
- Deck/story flow.
- Strong one-liner.
- Traction proof and gaps.
- Investor objections and answers.
- Milestones for the raise.
- Target investor profile.
- Outreach plan.

## Deck flow

Adapt, do not blindly copy:

1. Company purpose / one-line description.
2. Problem and customer pain.
3. Insight / why the old approach fails.
4. Solution.
5. Why now.
6. Market size and beachhead.
7. Product/demo.
8. Traction and customer proof.
9. Business model and GTM.
10. Competition and advantage.
11. Team.
12. Financials/milestones/use of funds.
13. Ask.

## Narrative rules

- The story must create belief in a large outcome, not just explain a product.
- The wedge must be narrow enough to be credible and big enough to expand.
- “Why now” should be based on a real shift: technology, regulation, cost curve, behavior, distribution, data, platform, urgency.
- Traction should be framed as evidence of a repeatable insight.
- Weaknesses should be addressed directly with milestones or proof plans.

## Metrics and proof

Use what fits the stage:

- Revenue/ARR/MRR growth.
- Retention, NRR, repeat usage.
- Activation and engagement.
- Pipeline, conversion, sales cycle.
- CAC/payback if meaningful.
- LOIs, pilots, design partners, waitlist quality.
- Customer quotes and case studies.
- Technical/product milestone.
- Team unfair advantage.

## High-upside moves

- Rewrite the pitch around the market insight, not the feature list.
- Show a wedge-to-platform expansion path.
- Build a “proof gap slide” internally: what investors will doubt and how to answer.
- Create a milestones-after-round slide that proves capital discipline.
- Tailor the same story to investor type without changing the core thesis.

## Failure modes

- Giant TAM with no beachhead.
- Product demo before problem urgency.
- “AI for X” or “Uber for Y” with no strategic insight.
- Weak team slide full of titles but no unfair advantage.
- Use of funds as a shopping list.

## Caution

Do not guarantee fundraising outcomes. If financial/legal/investment advice is requested, encourage professional review.
