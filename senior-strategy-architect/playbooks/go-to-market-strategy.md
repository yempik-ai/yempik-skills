# Go-To-Market Strategy Playbook

Use for launching, entering a market, moving from product to revenue, scaling a new offer, or aligning product,
marketing, sales, pricing, and customer success around a specific commercial motion.

## GTM definition

A GTM strategy answers: who we sell to, what pain we solve, why now, why us, how we reach and convert,
how we price/package, how we deliver value, and how we learn after launch.

## First diagnosis

Classify the GTM stage:

1. Pre-launch: need validation, beachhead, messaging, and early proof.
2. Early traction: need repeatable segment/channel/sales motion.
3. PMF to scale: need predictable acquisition, onboarding, retention, and unit economics.
4. Expansion: new segment/geography/channel requires adaptation.
5. Relaunch: old positioning or motion is not working.

Then identify the constraint: segment, pain urgency, proof, channel, sales motion, pricing, onboarding, or retention.

## Required strategic choices

- Beachhead segment: smallest segment where pain is acute and access is realistic.
- Use case wedge: the initial problem that opens the account/customer relationship.
- Differentiated promise: why this product is preferable to alternatives and status quo.
- GTM motion: founder-led, sales-led, PLG/self-serve, content-led, partner-led, community-led, marketplace, enterprise.
- Pricing and packaging: how value is captured and risk is reduced.
- Launch sequence: validation → beta/proof → demand capture → sales enablement → scale.
- Post-sale success: activation, adoption, retention, expansion.

## GTM motions

| Motion | Use when | Core proof |
|---|---|---|
| Founder-led sales | Early, complex, high-learning, high-trust | Direct customer insight and design partners |
| Sales-led | High ACV, complex buying committee, consultative sale | Pipeline, win rate, sales cycle, proof assets |
| Product-led | Product can show value quickly, low friction, broad user base | Activation, retention, expansion from usage |
| Content-led | Education creates demand and trust | Audience quality, leads, assisted revenue |
| Partner-led | Access/trust depends on ecosystem | Partner-sourced/influenced revenue |
| Community-led | Identity, expertise, support, or advocacy drives adoption | Member value, engagement, retention/referrals |
| Marketplace | Value depends on matching sides | Liquidity, time to match, repeat transactions |

## Output must include

- GTM diagnosis and stage.
- Beachhead ICP and use case.
- Positioning/narrative and proof needed.
- Motion choice and rejected alternatives.
- Pricing/packaging hypothesis.
- Launch plan by phase.
- Sales/marketing/customer success alignment.
- Feedback loop and success thresholds.
- Risks and kill criteria.

## 30/60/90 structure

### Days 0-30: Validate and sharpen
- Interview target buyers/users.
- Validate pain, urgency, alternatives, willingness to pay.
- Recruit design partners or first customers.
- Build landing page/message tests.
- Create proof assets.

### Days 31-60: Build repeatability
- Run one primary acquisition/sales motion.
- Track conversion by segment/message/channel.
- Standardize discovery, demo, onboarding, and objection handling.
- Refine pricing/packaging.

### Days 61-90: Scale what proves itself
- Double down on the highest-quality segment/channel.
- Add enablement and lifecycle systems.
- Expand only after activation and retention signals hold.

## Metrics

- Segment response rate.
- Qualified pipeline or qualified signups.
- Activation/adoption.
- Trial-to-paid or lead-to-customer conversion.
- Sales cycle and win rate.
- CAC, payback, ACV/ARPA.
- Retention, expansion, NRR.
- Customer proof created: case studies, testimonials, usage benchmarks.

## Failure modes

- Launching broad instead of beachhead-first.
- Confusing announcement with GTM.
- Scaling before messaging/channel/proof is repeatable.
- Ignoring onboarding and retention.
- Sales, marketing, and product telling different stories.
