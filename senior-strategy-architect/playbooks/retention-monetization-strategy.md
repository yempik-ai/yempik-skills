# Retention and Monetization Strategy Playbook

Use for churn, activation, engagement, lifecycle, customer success, loyalty, upsell, cross-sell,
NRR, repeat purchase, expansion, or monetization after acquisition.

## First diagnosis

Identify where value leaks:

- Acquisition quality: wrong customers are being acquired.
- Onboarding: customers do not reach first value quickly.
- Activation: users do not perform the key behavior that predicts retention.
- Habit/frequency: product is useful but not embedded.
- Outcome realization: customers do not see measurable progress.
- Support/friction: effort is too high.
- Expansion: usage grows but revenue does not.
- Pricing/packaging: monetization does not track value.
- Customer success: no health scoring or proactive intervention.

## Required strategic choices

- Retention segment: which customers/users matter most.
- Value moment: what behavior proves value.
- Activation path: shortest route to value.
- Engagement loop: what brings users back.
- Expansion path: how retained customers become more valuable.
- Lifecycle system: messages, triggers, interventions, success reviews.
- Metrics and health signals.

## Motions

| Motion | Use when | Key metric |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding redesign | Time-to-value is slow | Activation rate, time-to-value |
| Lifecycle messaging | Users need nudges/education | Activation, repeat usage, conversion |
| Customer success | High ACV or complex implementation | Renewal, expansion, health score |
| Product habit loop | Repeated usage drives value | Frequency, depth, cohort retention |
| Loyalty/referral | Repeat buyers and advocacy matter | Repeat purchase, redemption, referral |
| Expansion packaging | Usage/value grows after adoption | NRR, add-ons, tier upgrades |
| Win-back/resurrection | Dormant users can return | Resurrection rate, reactivation revenue |

## Output must include

- Retention/monetization diagnosis.
- Activation and value moments.
- Customer/user segments by retention quality.
- Lifecycle or success strategy.
- Expansion/monetization path.
- 30/60/90-day plan.
- Metrics and health score design.
- Risks and guardrails.

## Metrics

- Activation rate.
- Time-to-value.
- Cohort retention.
- Usage frequency/depth.
- Churn and contraction.
- NRR/GRR.
- Repeat purchase rate.
- Expansion revenue.
- Customer health score.
- Support tickets and friction signals.
- Referral/advocacy.

## High-upside moves

- Define the “aha” behavior and redesign onboarding to get there faster.
- Add customer-visible value reporting so users see ROI/progress.
- Use health-score triggers for proactive intervention before renewal risk.
- Create expansion moments tied to natural usage thresholds.
- Segment lifecycle by job and maturity, not by generic persona.

## Failure modes

- Treating retention as email reminders.
- Acquiring customers who were never a fit.
- Measuring logins instead of meaningful value behavior.
- Upselling before value is realized.
- Ignoring onboarding while blaming customer success.
