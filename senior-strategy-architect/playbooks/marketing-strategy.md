# Marketing Strategy Playbook

Use when the task involves demand creation, demand capture, campaigns, audience, channels, messaging,
performance marketing, brand/performance balance, or pipeline/revenue from marketing.

## First diagnosis

Identify the main marketing constraint:

1. Awareness: the right people do not know the brand exists.
2. Relevance: they know it but do not see why it matters now.
3. Trust: they are interested but do not believe the claim.
4. Conversion: the offer/funnel/sales handoff loses them.
5. Economics: acquisition works but CAC/payback/margin is weak.
6. Retention: marketing over-acquires because customer value leaks post-purchase.

Do not recommend channels until the constraint is named.

## Required strategic choices

- Target segment: who exactly, not “everyone interested in X.”
- Buying trigger: what moment makes them search, click, ask, buy, or switch.
- Core promise: what result or transformation is being sold.
- Proof: why the promise is believable.
- Channel role: demand creation, capture, nurture, conversion, retention, or proof.
- Offer: what makes action feel urgent and low-risk.
- Measurement: leading and lagging indicators.

## Useful lenses

- STP: segment, target, position.
- Jobs to Be Done: situation, trigger, desired progress, obstacle.
- Full funnel: awareness → consideration → conversion → retention/advocacy.
- Demand creation vs demand capture.
- Message-market fit before channel scale.
- Proof engine: case studies, demos, before/after, data, testimonials, founder credibility.

## Marketing motions

Choose one primary motion, then supporting motions.

| Motion | Best when | Watch out |
|---|---|---|
| Performance paid | Clear offer, measurable conversion, budget for testing | Scaling weak message wastes spend |
| SEO/demand capture | Existing search demand | Slow feedback; needs topical authority |
| Organic social/content | Strong POV, founder voice, visual proof, low budget | Vanity engagement without conversion path |
| Partnerships/influencers | Trust is borrowed from others | Bad fit damages credibility |
| Community | Audience needs identity, support, belonging, expertise | Hard to prove ROI if objective is vague |
| Lifecycle/CRM | Existing leads/users/customers under-monetized | Requires segmentation and data hygiene |
| PR/thought leadership | New category, credibility gap, strong story | Spikes without capture system vanish |
| Events/webinars | High-consideration B2B or expert-led services | Needs follow-up and sales integration |

## Output must include

- Marketing diagnosis.
- Target and buying trigger.
- Positioning/message angle.
- Channel architecture: primary channel, supporting channels, why each fits.
- Offer and conversion path.
- 30/60/90-day plan.
- Concrete campaign/content/message examples.
- Measurement dashboard.
- What not to do.

## Metrics

Pick by objective:

- Awareness: qualified reach, branded search, share of search, direct traffic, aided/unaided awareness.
- Demand capture: rankings, high-intent traffic, conversion rate, cost per qualified lead.
- Performance: CAC, MER, ROAS, payback, conversion rate, creative fatigue, landing page CVR.
- B2B pipeline: MQL→SQL, pipeline generated, win rate, sales cycle, ACV, sourced vs influenced revenue.
- Content/social: qualified engagement, saves/shares, email subscribers, lead quality, assisted conversions.
- Retention/lifecycle: activation, repeat purchase, churn, expansion, NRR, referral rate.

## High-upside moves

Use only if realistic:

- Create a “proof asset” that sales and marketing can both use: teardown, calculator, benchmark, audit, demo, mini-report.
- Pick one painful enemy: wasted time, expensive agencies, hidden fees, boring category, risky compliance, status quo.
- Build a wedge campaign around a neglected micro-segment instead of broad generic positioning.
- Turn objections into content: each sales objection becomes a landing page, email, short video, and sales proof point.
- Use a flagship offer to create urgency: diagnostic, challenge, benchmark, template, trial, migration, bundle, or guarantee.

## Failure modes

- Channel salad: trying every channel without a core motion.
- Brand/performance split: awareness efforts not connected to conversion or revenue learning.
- Generic value proposition: claims that competitors could copy word-for-word.
- Weak proof: testimonials or claims that do not reduce perceived risk.
- Optimizing last-click metrics while ignoring long buying journeys.
