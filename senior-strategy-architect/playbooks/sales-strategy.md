# Sales Strategy Playbook

Use for outbound, inbound sales, pipeline design, qualification, demos, discovery, enterprise sales,
negotiation, sales scripts, sales enablement, buyer journey, or revenue team process.

## First diagnosis

Identify where revenue is leaking:

- ICP: selling to the wrong accounts/customers.
- Prospecting: insufficient qualified conversations.
- Qualification: poor fit consumes pipeline.
- Discovery: seller misses pain, urgency, decision process, or value.
- Demo/proposal: product is shown before the buyer's problem is framed.
- Trust/proof: buyer fears risk.
- Business case: value is not quantified.
- Buying committee: champions cannot sell internally.
- Process: no stage exit criteria, follow-up, or forecast discipline.
- Pricing/discounting: price becomes the only lever.

## Required strategic choices

- ICP/account segment.
- Buyer roles: economic buyer, champion, user, blocker, technical/legal/procurement.
- Sales motion: founder-led, SDR/AE, inbound, outbound, channel, enterprise, land-and-expand.
- Qualification criteria.
- Pipeline stages and exit criteria.
- Messaging and objection handling.
- Enablement assets.
- Forecast and coaching cadence.

## B2B buyer journey lens

For complex B2B, map buying jobs:

- Problem identification.
- Solution exploration.
- Requirements building.
- Supplier selection.
- Internal consensus and risk management.

Sales strategy should reduce buyer effort and increase confidence at each job.

## Output must include

- Sales diagnosis.
- ICP and trigger events.
- Sales motion and why.
- Pipeline architecture.
- Discovery questions.
- Messaging/sequence/demo guidance.
- Objection handling.
- Enablement assets.
- Metrics dashboard.
- 30/60/90-day sales execution plan.

## Pipeline stage design

Each stage needs:

- Buyer commitment required to enter.
- Exit criteria.
- Required data fields.
- Next action.
- Common failure.
- Coaching question.

Avoid stages that describe seller activity only, such as “sent proposal,” unless paired with buyer commitment.

## Metrics

- Qualified account list size.
- Connect/reply/meeting rate.
- SQL/opportunity conversion.
- Pipeline coverage.
- Stage conversion.
- Win rate.
- ACV/average order value.
- Sales cycle.
- Discount rate.
- Churn/retention by acquisition source.
- Forecast accuracy.

## High-upside moves

- Build a “buyer confidence kit”: ROI proof, implementation plan, security FAQ, case study, objection answers.
- Use trigger-based outbound instead of generic persona-based outbound.
- Rewrite demos around the buyer’s current pain and desired business outcome.
- Create a champion enablement one-pager they can forward internally.
- Disqualify faster to protect sales capacity.

## Failure modes

- Bigger pipeline full of weak-fit deals.
- Sales scripts that ignore buyer context.
- Demoing features before diagnosing pain.
- Discounting instead of proving value.
- No stage exit criteria.
