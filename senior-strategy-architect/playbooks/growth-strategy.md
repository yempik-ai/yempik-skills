# Growth Strategy Playbook

Use for acquisition, activation, retention, referral, monetization, experiment systems, growth loops,
channel-market fit, and scalable user/customer growth.

## First diagnosis

Start with the growth equation. Find the current bottleneck:

- Acquisition: not enough qualified users/leads.
- Activation: people arrive but do not reach value.
- Retention: people try but do not return or renew.
- Referral/virality: users do not bring others.
- Revenue: usage exists but monetization is weak.
- Economics: growth is unprofitable or fragile.
- Loop weakness: outputs do not reinvest into inputs.

Never optimize acquisition before checking activation and retention.

## Required strategic choices

- North Star: the metric that connects customer value and business value.
- Primary bottleneck: one stage/loop to improve first.
- Growth model: funnel, loop, sales motion, content engine, paid engine, marketplace liquidity, community, partnerships.
- Experiment cadence: how many tests, who owns them, how decisions are made.
- Guardrails: retention, margin, customer quality, brand risk.

## Growth loop design

Describe loops as:

1. Input: what starts the loop?
2. Action: what does the user/company do?
3. Output: what asset/result is created?
4. Reinvestment: how does the output create more input?
5. Constraint: what limits the loop?
6. Metric: how is loop efficiency measured?

Examples of loop types:

- Content loop: insights/content → search/social discovery → audience/leads → data/questions → better content.
- Referral loop: user value → invite/share → new user → value delivered → more invites.
- Collaboration loop: user creates asset → invites collaborators → collaborators become users.
- Marketplace loop: more supply → better demand experience → more demand → better supply economics.
- Paid loop: CAC-positive acquisition → revenue/payback → reinvestment into acquisition.
- Community loop: member contributions → content/support/value → more members → more contributions.

## Experiment system

Each experiment needs:

- Hypothesis.
- Segment.
- Action.
- Metric.
- Threshold.
- Duration.
- Decision: scale, iterate, stop.

Use ICE/RICE when prioritizing but do not fake precision. Explain confidence.

## Output must include

- Growth diagnosis.
- Growth model and North Star.
- Bottleneck and why it matters.
- 3-5 growth bets ranked by expected leverage.
- Experiment backlog.
- 30-day sprint plan.
- Metrics dashboard.
- Loop or funnel map.
- Risks and guardrails.

## Metrics

- Acquisition: qualified traffic, signups, leads, CAC, channel conversion, payback.
- Activation: time-to-value, onboarding completion, key action completion, activation rate.
- Retention: cohort retention, repeat usage, churn, resurrection, customer health.
- Referral: invite rate, acceptance rate, viral coefficient, share-to-signup conversion.
- Revenue: conversion to paid, ARPA, expansion, NRR, LTV, margin.
- Loop: output/input ratio, reinvestment rate, cycle time, constraint.

## High-upside moves

- Shift from campaign thinking to a compounding loop.
- Build a “growth wedge” around the smallest audience with highest activation.
- Fix time-to-value before adding more acquisition spend.
- Use product usage data to create lifecycle and sales triggers.
- Turn the best customer outcome into a repeatable acquisition asset.

## Failure modes

- Growth hacks without retention.
- Optimizing vanity metrics.
- Too many experiments with no learning standard.
- Channel tests that fail because the offer/message is weak.
- Scaling paid acquisition before payback is understood.
