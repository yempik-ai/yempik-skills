# Content Strategy Playbook

Use for organic content, SEO, social, newsletters, thought leadership, creator strategy, personal brand,
editorial calendar, content-led growth, or sales-enablement content.

## First diagnosis

Identify the content job:

- Demand creation: make the target care about a problem.
- Demand capture: rank/appear when the target is already searching.
- Trust building: prove expertise and reduce perceived risk.
- Sales enablement: answer objections and support buying committees.
- Retention/community: help users get value and stay engaged.
- Founder/personal brand: build authority and access.

Then diagnose the constraint:

- Weak POV.
- Wrong audience.
- Generic topics.
- No conversion path.
- No distribution.
- No proof.
- Inconsistent production system.
- Content attracts the wrong customer.

## Required strategic choices

- Audience and job.
- Point of view: what do we believe that others understate or get wrong?
- Content pillars.
- Formats and channels.
- Distribution system.
- Conversion path.
- Repurposing workflow.
- Measurement by content job.

## Content architecture

Create three layers:

1. Flagship content: deep assets that define authority.
2. Recurring content: weekly formats that build familiarity.
3. Conversion content: landing pages, case studies, comparison pages, objection handling, emails.

## Output must include

- Content diagnosis.
- Audience and content job.
- POV and editorial thesis.
- Pillars and formats.
- Distribution plan.
- 30-day content calendar with example titles/hooks.
- Conversion path.
- Metrics.
- What not to create.

## Metrics

Choose by objective:

- SEO: qualified organic traffic, rankings for intent clusters, assisted conversions.
- Social: saves, shares, profile visits, qualified inbound, subscriber growth.
- Newsletter: subscriber quality, open/click, replies, meetings/revenue assisted.
- Sales enablement: asset usage, objection reduction, deal velocity, win rate.
- Trust: case study views, demo requests, branded search, direct traffic.

## High-upside moves

- Build an “objection library”: one asset per sales objection.
- Create a flagship benchmark/report/calculator that others cite.
- Own a contrarian but defensible category point of view.
- Turn customer calls into content themes and proof assets.
- Use one deep weekly asset to generate multiple short-form posts, email, sales snippets, and landing page sections.

## Failure modes

- Content calendar with no strategic job.
- Thought leadership with no thought.
- SEO traffic that attracts non-buyers.
- Social engagement with no conversion path.
- Publishing frequency treated as strategy.
