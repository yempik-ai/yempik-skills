# Pricing Strategy Playbook

Use for pricing level, packaging, discounting, value metric, tiers, freemium, usage pricing,
subscription models, services pricing, marketplace take rate, or monetization strategy.

## First diagnosis

Identify the pricing problem:

- Price metric mismatch: charging for the wrong unit of value.
- Packaging confusion: buyers cannot choose easily.
- Segment mismatch: different willingness-to-pay segments forced into one price.
- Value proof gap: price feels high because value is not quantified.
- Margin issue: price does not cover delivery, support, CAC, or risk.
- Discount leakage: sales uses discounting to solve positioning/trust issues.
- Conversion issue: risk/friction too high, not necessarily price too high.
- Monetization timing: charging too early/late in the value journey.

## Required strategic choices

- Pricing objective: adoption, revenue, margin, premium positioning, penetration, expansion, retention.
- Value metric: seat, usage, volume, outcome, GMV, credits, feature access, project, retainer, transaction.
- Segmentation: who pays more and why.
- Packaging: tiers, bundles, add-ons, limits, fences.
- Anchor: next-best alternative, ROI, status quo cost, competitor, budget category.
- Discount rules: when and why discounts are allowed.
- Test plan: how to validate without damaging trust.

## Pricing approaches

| Approach | Use when | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Value-based | Value can be quantified or strongly perceived | Needs proof and customer research |
| Cost-plus | Costs are clear and margin protection matters | Ignores willingness to pay and competitive context |
| Competitive | Category has known anchors | Can trap you in commodity pricing |
| Penetration | Adoption/network effects matter | Can attract low-quality customers |
| Premium/skimming | Differentiation/proof/status is strong | Narrows market and raises expectations |
| Freemium | Product has low marginal cost and upgrade path | Free users can become support burden |
| Usage-based | Value scales with consumption | Revenue predictability and bill shock risk |
| Tiered | Segments have distinct needs/WTP | Too many tiers create confusion |

## Output must include

- Pricing diagnosis.
- Customer value and next-best alternative.
- Recommended pricing model and value metric.
- Packaging/tier proposal.
- Discounting and negotiation rules.
- Rollout/testing plan.
- Metrics and guardrails.
- Risks and customer communication.

## Metrics

- Conversion rate by segment/tier.
- ARPA/ARPU/ACV.
- Gross margin and contribution margin.
- CAC payback.
- Retention/churn by plan.
- Expansion and NRR.
- Discount rate.
- Sales cycle impact.
- Support cost by tier.
- Price objection frequency.

## High-upside moves

- Shift from feature-based tiers to outcome/value-based packages.
- Add a high-anchor premium tier to reveal willingness to pay and reframe value.
- Create a migration path that protects existing customers while testing new pricing.
- Use packaging fences instead of discounts to protect price integrity.
- Add a risk-reversal mechanism: pilot, guarantee, implementation support, proof milestone.

## Failure modes

- Treating price as a number instead of a positioning and value-capture system.
- Changing price without changing packaging/proof.
- Copying competitor pricing without understanding their economics.
- Running discounts that train customers to wait.
- Pricing below cost of service or support.
