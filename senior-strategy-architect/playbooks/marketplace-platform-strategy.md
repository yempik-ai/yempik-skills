# Marketplace and Platform Strategy Playbook

Use for two-sided marketplaces, platforms, network effects, liquidity, cold start, supply/demand seeding,
trust and safety, take rate, or ecosystem/platform business models.

## First diagnosis

Identify the platform constraint:

- Cold start: neither side joins without the other.
- Supply constraint: buyers exist but supply is thin, low quality, or unreliable.
- Demand constraint: supply exists but buyers are not converting.
- Liquidity problem: matches/searches fail or take too long.
- Trust/safety problem: users fear fraud, quality, or risk.
- Unit economics: incentives/subsidies/take rate break margin.
- Fragmentation: marketplace is too broad to create density.
- Disintermediation: users bypass the platform after matching.

## Required strategic choices

- Atomic network: smallest segment/geography/use case where liquidity can be high.
- Side to seed first: supply, demand, or managed transaction.
- Liquidity metric: time to match, fill rate, search success, booking rate, utilization.
- Trust mechanism: identity, reviews, guarantees, insurance, curation, dispute resolution, payments.
- Monetization: take rate, subscription, listing fee, lead fee, SaaS, ads, financing, services.
- Growth loop: how more activity improves the marketplace.
- Governance: quality standards, incentives, fraud prevention, disintermediation controls.

## Cold-start patterns

- Single-player utility before marketplace.
- Managed marketplace: manually coordinate early transactions.
- Constrain geography/category/use case.
- Seed the harder side with incentives or guaranteed value.
- Aggregate supply first if buyers need choice.
- Aggregate demand first if suppliers are hard to motivate without proof.
- Use a wedge community or expert curation.
- Start with high-frequency/high-urgency use cases.

## Output must include

- Marketplace diagnosis.
- Atomic network definition.
- Supply/demand strategy.
- Liquidity target and metrics.
- Trust/safety system.
- Monetization/take-rate hypothesis.
- Growth loop.
- 30/60/90-day plan.
- Risks and disintermediation prevention.

## Metrics

- Liquidity: search-to-match, time to match, fill rate, booking rate.
- Supply: active supply, utilization, response time, quality, churn.
- Demand: active buyers, repeat transactions, conversion, CAC.
- Economics: GMV, take rate, contribution margin, subsidy cost, payback.
- Trust: cancellations, disputes, fraud, refunds, review quality.
- Network: density by segment/geography, repeat matches, cross-side retention.

## High-upside moves

- Own one atomic network completely before expanding.
- Use concierge/manual operations to fake liquidity until patterns are clear.
- Build trust as a product feature, not a policy page.
- Create supply tools that are valuable even before demand is abundant.
- Use pricing/subsidies only where they create repeat liquidity, not vanity volume.

## Failure modes

- Chasing total users instead of successful matches.
- Expanding geography/category before density.
- Subsidizing transactions with no path to natural liquidity.
- Ignoring trust and quality.
- Choosing take rate before understanding value and disintermediation risk.
