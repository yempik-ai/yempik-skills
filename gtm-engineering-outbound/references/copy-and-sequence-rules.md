# Copy And Sequence Rules

Use this for email, LinkedIn, follow-ups, and message critique.

## Copy Formula

Specific signal -> plausible pain or opportunity -> simple value -> soft CTA.

Example structure:

```text
Saw [specific signal].
Teams in that situation often run into [pain].
We help [ICP] get [outcome] without [friction/risk].
Worth a quick look?
```

## Rules

- Keep cold emails short.
- Use one strong signal, not five weak facts.
- Do not outsource the whole email to AI from generic inputs.
- Let AI help with research, classification, variants, and cleanup.
- Human decides angle, claim, proof, and offer.
- Write like a person who understands the prospect's context.
- Avoid fake familiarity.
- Avoid generic praise about mission, website, LinkedIn, or "impressive growth."
- Do not mention scraped facts if they feel creepy or irrelevant.

## First Email

Goal: earn a reply, not explain everything.

Must contain:

- one concrete observation;
- one pain/opportunity linked to that observation;
- one clear outcome;
- one low-friction question.

Avoid:

- long company intros;
- feature dumps;
- multiple CTAs;
- "I hope this email finds you well";
- "I noticed your company is doing amazing things."

## Follow-Up

Default: one light follow-up after about 4 days, unless the user's workflow specifies otherwise.

Good follow-up jobs:

- ask a simpler question;
- add one useful clarification;
- offer a binary path;
- route to the right person;
- keep tone human and non-pushy.

## Nurturing

If a prospect is interested but not ready:

- log timing and reason;
- send useful context, not pressure;
- create a reminder or sequence separate from cold outreach;
- preserve the original signal and pain context.

## LinkedIn Support

Use LinkedIn when:

- email deliverability is uncertain;
- buyer is active on LinkedIn;
- community/follower/event signal is part of the list source;
- the offer requires trust before email conversion.

Do not paste the same cold email into LinkedIn. Make it shorter and more conversational.

## Example Templates

### Signal-Based Email

```text
Subject: [short reference to signal or outcome]

Hi {{first_name}},

I saw {{specific_signal}}.

Usually that creates {{pain_or_opportunity}} for {{buyer/team}}.

We help {{ICP}} get {{outcome}} by {{simple_mechanism}}.

Worth checking if this is relevant for {{company}}?
```

### Light Follow-Up

```text
Hi {{first_name}},

Quick follow-up on this.

Is {{pain_or_goal}} something your team is looking at now, or is it not a priority?
```

### Referral Ask

```text
Hi {{first_name}},

Not sure you own this.

Who is the right person for {{topic/problem}} at {{company}}?
```

## QA Checklist

- Would the message still make sense if the signal were removed? If yes, it is too generic.
- Does the signal justify the pain? If no, rebuild the angle.
- Is the claim believable with current proof? If no, soften it.
- Is the CTA easy to answer? If no, simplify.
- Could this be sent to 1,000 random companies? If yes, it is not specific enough.
