# GTM Engineering Intake Questionnaire

Use this when the request lacks enough context to design a reliable outbound system.
Ask only the questions needed. Prefer 3-5 questions unless doing a full onboarding.

## Minimum Intake

1. What is the offer, and what specific outcome does it create?
2. Who is the first ICP you want to target? Include exclusions.
3. What painful problem or urgency makes them likely to care now?
4. What proof do you have: customers, case studies, numbers, testimonials, internal data?
5. What channels/tools/infrastructure already exist?

## Full Intake

### Offer

- Product/service name.
- Category and simple explanation.
- Main value proposition.
- Typical pricing or ACV range if known.
- Delivery model: self-serve, service, SaaS, consulting, agency, hybrid.

### ICP: Company

- Industry.
- Geography.
- Company size.
- Revenue/funding/stage.
- Business model.
- Technology stack.
- Maturity indicators.
- Exclusions: who should never enter the list.

### ICP: Person

- Job titles.
- Seniority.
- Department/function.
- Buying influence: user, champion, budget owner, executive sponsor.
- LinkedIn/community/event/source indicators.

### Pain And Urgency

- Pain points solved.
- Why now.
- What happens if they do nothing.
- Existing alternatives or status quo.
- Competitors or adjacent tools/services.

### Proof

- Customer names or anonymized examples.
- Before/after metrics.
- Case studies.
- Testimonials.
- Internal benchmarks.
- Founder/operator credibility.

### Signals

- Observable buying triggers.
- Data sources where these triggers can be found.
- Signals that indicate bad fit.
- Signals that change the message angle.

### Channels

- Email.
- LinkedIn.
- Phone.
- Partnerships/community.
- Paid or retargeting support.
- Existing newsletter/audience.

### Delivery Infrastructure

- Sending domains.
- Mailboxes per domain.
- Domain age.
- Warmup status.
- Sequencer used.
- Daily volume.
- Recent bounce, spam, reply, and positive reply rates.

### Goals

- Booked meetings.
- Qualified opportunities.
- Revenue/pipeline.
- Customer interviews.
- Waitlist/beta.
- Partner conversations.

### Constraints

- Budget.
- Timeframe.
- Team capacity.
- Geography/language.
- Legal/compliance constraints.
- Data source limitations.
